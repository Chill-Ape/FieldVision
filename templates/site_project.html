NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) measures vegetation health by analyzing chlorophyll absorption. Higher values (green) indicate healthier vegetation, while lower values (red/brown) may indicate stress, disease, or sparse vegetation.
NDRE (Normalized Difference Red Edge) is sensitive to chlorophyll content and nitrogen levels in plants. It's particularly useful for detecting early stress and optimizing fertilizer application.
Moisture Index (NDMI) estimates water content in vegetation and soil. This helps identify irrigation needs and monitor drought stress in crops.
EVI (Enhanced Vegetation Index) provides improved sensitivity in high biomass regions and better vegetation monitoring through reduced atmospheric influences.
NDWI (Normalized Difference Water Index) detects water bodies and monitors water content in vegetation. Useful for flood mapping and irrigation management.
Chlorophyll Index tracks chlorophyll concentration in leaves, providing insights into plant health and photosynthetic activity.
True Color Satellite Image provides visual context for your field analysis, showing the actual appearance of your field from space using red, green, and blue light bands.
